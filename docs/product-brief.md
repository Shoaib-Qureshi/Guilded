# Product Brief

Source: `Guilded - Tech doc.docx`. This is the "why" behind everything in the other docs.

## Name

Guilded — a play on "Guild" (a trade association of peers) and "Gilded" (premium/exclusive).

## Problem statement

Small to mid-sized agency founders struggle to find trusted, relevant peer support. Existing networking environments are broad and fragmented — a founder needs a curated digital space for confidential, high-quality peer interaction within their niche.

## USP

1. Human/expert moderation — not an unmoderated forum.
2. Conversational learning / peer support, not passive content consumption.
3. Relevant networking — being around like-minded people leads to better problem-solving.
4. Digital networking is scalable and convenient.
5. Not region- or niche-specific.

## What Guilded is not doing

No freelancers, no employees, no interns. Members are more likely to share sensitive information (pricing, client struggles, hiring failures) when they know everyone in the room is a true peer — an agency owner/operator — not a potential competitor's employee. This exclusion is the basis of the eligibility gate in `roles-and-permissions.md` and `rules-and-regulations.md`.

## Who it's for

Agency founders/owners who:
- Work at an agency with 2–50 employees.
- Hold an operational role (not just an investor or figurehead).
- Are not freelancer-only.

Access is invite-only or through application — see `user-flow.md` and `moderator-flow.md` for the full vetting pipeline.
